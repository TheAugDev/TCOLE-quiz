Child Safety Check Alert List (CSCAL)

A Law Enforcement Guide

CSCAL Basics

Purpose and Definition +

Family Code 261.3022. Child Safety Check Alert List

The Child Safety Check Alert List (CSCAL) is a method for Child Protective Services (CPS) to report the names of children and their legal guardians whom they are attempting to locate. It is submitted to the Texas Crime Information Center (TCIC).

The Purpose of CSCAL is to locate a child and guardian to:

  • Investigate a report of child abuse or neglect.
  • Provide protective services to a family receiving family-based safety services.
  • Provide services to the family of a child in the managing conservatorship of DFPS.

CSCAL vs. Missing Person Report

Child Safety Check Alert List (CSCAL)

  • The child is believed to be **with** a legal guardian.
  • Entered into **TCIC only** (Texas statewide system).
  • Remains in TCIC for **12 months**.

Missing Person Report

  • The legal guardian **does not know** the location of the child.
  • Entered into **NCIC** (national system).
  • Remains in the system **indefinitely** until located.
DFPS Duty to Notify Law Enforcement+

Family Code 261.301. Investigation of Report

This statute provides guidelines for joint investigations between DFPS and law enforcement. A recent update added a key requirement for high-priority cases.

If, during a highest-priority investigation, DFPS is unable to locate the child or family, DFPS **shall notify the Department of Public Safety (DPS)** that their location is unknown. If DPS later locates the family, they must notify DFPS.

Practical Implication: This creates a formal communication loop. DFPS can't find a child in a critical case, so they alert DPS. This may lead to the creation of a CSCAL entry, allowing any officer in the state who contacts the family to close that loop and inform DFPS of their location.

Officer Encounter Protocol

Initial Steps & Authority to Detain +

CCP Art. 2A.056. Response to Child Safety Check Alert

When an officer encounters a person listed on the CSCAL, they must perform several actions to assess the child's well-being and report the contact.

Immediate Officer Actions Checklist:

  • **Immediately contact DFPS** on their dedicated law enforcement telephone number for statewide intake.
  • **Request information from DFPS** regarding the circumstances of the case.
  • **Request information from the child and adult** regarding the child's safety, well-being, and current residence.
Practical Example: An officer makes a traffic stop. The driver is a positive TCIC hit for a CSCAL entry with her child. The officer's first step is to call the DFPS LE hotline. While on the phone, the officer can ask the driver for her current address and observe the child for any immediate signs of distress or injury, relaying this information to the DFPS intake specialist.

Authority to Temporarily Detain

Under CCP Art. 2A.056(d), a peace officer **may temporarily detain the child or other person** to ensure the safety and well-being of the child.

This detention is for the purpose of the on-scene investigation and communication with DFPS. The CPS caseworker will decide whether to respond to the location. Regardless of the CPS response, the officer should assess the immediate safety of the child.

Taking Temporary Possession of a Child

Emergency Possession Without a Court Order +

Family Code 262.104. Taking Possession in Emergency

An **authorized representative of DFPS, a law enforcement officer, or a juvenile probation officer** may take temporary possession of a child without a court order **only if** there is no time to obtain one and certain conditions are met. The basis for this belief can be from personal knowledge or from information furnished by another person that has been corroborated by the officer's personal knowledge.

Conditions for Warrantless Possession:

  • There is an **immediate danger** to the physical health or safety of the child.
  • The child has been a victim of **sexual abuse** or **human trafficking** (PC 20A.02 or 20A.03).
  • The parent/guardian is currently using a controlled substance that constitutes an immediate danger to the child.
  • The parent/guardian has allowed the child to remain on premises used for the **manufacture of methamphetamine**.
Practical Example: During a CSCAL encounter, an officer observes that the child appears malnourished and has visible, unexplained bruising. The parent seems heavily intoxicated. Based on these facts, the officer believes there is an immediate danger to the child's physical health and can take temporary possession under this statute without first obtaining a warrant.

Family Code 262.105. Filing Petition After Possession

If an officer takes possession of a child under the emergency circumstances of FC 262.104, a formal petition must be filed with the court, initiating legal proceedings.

Release & Reporting Procedures

Release of a Child +

CCP Art. 2A.058. Release of Child by Law Enforcement

An officer who takes possession of a child may release that child to one of several authorized entities.

Authorized Release Options:

  • A licensed residential child-care facility.
  • A juvenile probation department.
  • The Department of Family and Protective Services (DFPS).
  • Any other person authorized by law to take possession of the child (e.g., a non-offending parent or relative).

Checklist Before Releasing to a Person

Before releasing a child to a person (other than a government entity like DFPS), an officer **must** complete the following checks:

Required Verification Steps:

  • Verify with **NCIC** that the child is not a missing child.
  • Search NCIC databases for protection orders, warrants, and sex offender registries for the person taking custody.
  • Call the **DFPS Texas Abuse Hotline** to check if the person is in the child abuse/neglect registry.
  • Verify the person is at least **18 years of age**.
  • Maintain a record of the placement.
Reporting Requirements After Encounter+

CCP Art. 2A.056(d) & (e). Reporting

After an encounter, specific reporting is required to both DFPS and TCIC to clear the alert.

Post-Encounter Reporting Checklist:

  • **If you DO NOT take possession of the child:** You must obtain the child's current address and any other relevant information and **report it to DFPS**.
  • **After ANY encounter:** You must report to the **Texas Crime Information Center (TCIC)** that the person has been located and to whom the child was released (if applicable).
Practical Example: After the encounter from the previous tab, the officer determines there is no immediate danger and leaves the child with the parent. The officer must first report the child's current address and status to DFPS. Then, the officer must make a report/notification to their dispatch or records division to have the CSCAL entry cleared from TCIC, noting the child was located and left with the parent.
