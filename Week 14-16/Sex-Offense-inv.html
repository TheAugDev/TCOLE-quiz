Sexual Offenses

A Comprehensive Penal Code Study Guide

Victimology & Trauma-Informed Response

Dynamics of Sexual Assault, Consent & Reporting Barriers

Core Dynamics & Consent

  • Sexual assault is a hostile act of control, domination, and humiliation, not sexual desire.
  • **Consent** means "assent in fact, whether expressed or apparent." It must be knowing and voluntary.
  • **It is NOT consent if:** A sexual act is accomplished through coercion, force, or threats; the victim is silent; the victim is unconscious, physically unable to resist, or lacks mental capacity; or the victim is intoxicated and unaware. Any sexual act with a victim under 17 is nonconsensual by law (subject to affirmative defenses).

Barriers to Reporting

Victims/survivors may not report due to:

  • Shame, guilt, or self-blame.
  • Fear of not being believed, societal "rape myths" (e.g., blaming attire or alcohol use).
  • Betrayal of trust, especially in non-stranger assaults.
  • Fear of facing the offender again (at school, work, etc.).
  • Concerns about how law enforcement will react ("secondary victimization").
Trauma & Neurobiological Response

How Trauma Affects the Brain & Body

  • During a traumatic event, a flood of hormones (like cortisol and adrenaline) is released to help the person survive.
  • These hormones impact demeanor, causing a wide range of reactions: apathy, crying, laughter, anger, or appearing frozen/dazed. **There is no "correct" reaction.**
  • These same hormones disrupt how memories are stored, causing them to be fragmented and non-linear. A survivor may recall sensory details (a smell, the ceiling) vividly but not the sequence of events.
  • A sexual response (arousal, orgasm) can be an involuntary physiological reaction and **does not indicate consent**.

Trauma-Informed Approach: The 4 R's

  • Realize: Understand the widespread impact of trauma.
  • Recognize: Know the signs and symptoms of trauma.
  • Respond: Integrate knowledge about trauma into policies and procedures.
  • Resist Re-traumatization: Proactively prevent further harm during the investigation.
Strangulation & High-Risk Populations

Strangulation: Lethality Predictor

Strangulation is one of the most accurate predictors of homicide.

"If a man applies pressure to a woman's neck one time in an intimate relationship, she is 750% more likely to later be killed by him."

**Important:** Strangulation is intentional; choking is accidental. A victim indicating they were "choked" must be medically evaluated, as serious internal injury or death can occur even without visible external signs.

Considerations for High-Risk Populations

Be mindful of unique barriers for populations such as children, the elderly, immigrants, LGBTQ+ individuals, males, people with disabilities, and sex workers. This includes being aware of personal biases, using appropriate terminology, understanding cultural contexts, and connecting victims with specialized resources.

Adult Offenses

Assaultive Offenses

22.011. Sexual Assault

A person intentionally or knowingly causes the penetration of the anus or sexual organ of another by any means, or the mouth by the actor's sexual organ, without the other person's consent.

Punishment: 2nd Degree Felony

Elements Checklist:

  • Did the actor act `intentionally or knowingly`?
  • Was there `penetration` of the anus, sexual organ, or mouth?
  • Was it `without consent`? (Consent is absent due to force, threats, incapacitation, or exploitation by a person in a position of trust like a doctor, clergyman, coach, or caregiver).
Example: A psychiatrist (Dr. Phil) convinces his patient (Dee) that having sex with him is part of her therapy to overcome trauma. Because he is exploiting her emotional dependency as a mental health provider, her submission does not constitute legal consent. This is Sexual Assault.

22.021. Aggravated Sexual Assault

A person commits Sexual Assault and causes serious bodily injury; places the victim in fear of death, SBI, or kidnapping; uses or exhibits a deadly weapon; or the victim is elderly or disabled.

Punishment: 1st Degree Felony

Elements Checklist:

  • Did the actor commit the elements of `Sexual Assault`? AND
  • Did one of the aggravating factors exist? (e.g., caused `Serious Bodily Injury`, used a `deadly weapon`, etc.)
Example: Dr. Phil gives Dee water with a Xanax tablet in it without her knowledge, causing her to become groggy. He then has intercourse with her. Because he administered a substance to impair her ability to resist, this elevates the crime to Aggravated Sexual Assault.

22.012. Indecent Assault

Without consent and with intent to arouse or gratify sexual desire, a person touches the anus, breast, or genitals of another; exposes their genitals; or causes contact with bodily fluids.

Punishment: Class A Misdemeanor

Elements Checklist:

  • Did the actor act `without consent`?
  • Did they have the `intent to arouse or gratify` sexual desire?
  • Did they touch an intimate part, expose themselves, or cause contact with fluids?
Example: Seymour, 17, approaches Ann, 16, at her locker. He runs his hands under her shirt and squeezes her breasts. Ann pushes him away. This is Indecent Assault because the touching was nonconsensual and done with sexual intent.

Child Offenses

Offenses Against Children

21.11. Indecency with a Child

With a child younger than 17, a person engages in sexual contact with the child OR, with intent to arouse, exposes their genitals to the child.

Punishment: 2nd Degree Felony (for contact) / 3rd Degree Felony (for exposure)

Elements Checklist:

  • Is the victim a `child` (under 17)?
  • Did the actor engage in `sexual contact` with the child? OR
  • With `intent to arouse`, did the actor `expose` their genitals to the child?
Example: Forrest, 18, babysits his 13-year-old sister, Jenny. For two weeks, they engage in mutual genital touching. Forrest has committed Indecency with a Child.

22.011(a)(2). Sexual Assault of a Child

A person intentionally or knowingly causes the penetration of the anus, sexual organ, or mouth of a child (under 17).

Punishment: 2nd Degree Felony

Elements Checklist:

  • Is the victim a `child` (under 17)?
  • Did the actor `intentionally or knowingly` cause `penetration` of the child's anus, sexual organ, or mouth?
Example: After two weeks of touching, Forrest and Jenny engage in sexual intercourse. Forrest has now committed Sexual Assault of a Child. Note: The "Romeo and Juliet" affirmative defense may apply if the actor is not more than 3 years older than the victim (who must be 14+), was not a registered sex offender, and the relationship was not otherwise prohibited.

22.021(a)(2)(B). Aggravated Sexual Assault of a Child

A person commits Sexual Assault and the victim is younger than 14.

Punishment: 1st Degree Felony (with a minimum 25-year sentence if the victim is under 6, or under 14 with SBI/threats).

Elements Checklist:

  • Did the actor commit `Sexual Assault`?
  • Was the victim `younger than 14`?
Example: Forrest and Jenny engage in intercourse with Candy, who is 12. Because Candy is under 14, Forrest has committed Aggravated Sexual Assault.

21.02. Continuous Sexual Abuse of Young Child or Children

During a period of 30 days or more, a person (17 or older) commits two or more acts of sexual abuse against a child younger than 14.

Punishment: 1st Degree Felony (Life or 25-99 years)

Elements Checklist:

  • Did the acts occur over a period of `30 days or more`?
  • Were there `two or more` acts of sexual abuse (e.g., Sexual Assault, Indecency)?
  • Was the actor `17 or older`?
  • Was the victim `younger than 14`?
Example: Forrest (18) has intercourse with Jenny (13) twice a week for three weeks. Because this involves two or more acts of sexual abuse against a child under 14 over a 30+ day period (or close to it), he has committed this offense.

25.02. Prohibited Sexual Conduct

A person engages in sexual intercourse or deviate sexual intercourse with another person the actor knows to be within a prohibited degree of relationship (e.g., ancestor, descendant, sibling, aunt/uncle, niece/nephew, first cousin).

Punishment: 3rd Degree Felony (enhanced to 2nd Degree for ancestor/descendant).

Elements Checklist:

  • Did the person engage in `sexual intercourse` or `deviate sexual intercourse`?
  • Did they `know` the other person was within a prohibited degree of `consanguinity` (blood relationship)?
Example: Chase (16) and Candy (14) are first cousins. When they have sex, they are committing Prohibited Sexual Conduct.
Educator & Online Offenses

21.12. Improper Relationship Between Educator and Student

An employee of a school engages in sexual contact or intercourse with a student enrolled at that school or a student they know is enrolled in any school.

Punishment: 2nd Degree Felony

Elements Checklist:

  • Is the actor an `employee` of a primary or secondary school?
  • Did they engage in `sexual contact` or `intercourse`?
  • Was the other person a `student` enrolled at the school where the employee works OR a student the employee otherwise knew was enrolled in a school?
Example: Apera, a 20-year-old teaching intern, has a sexual relationship with Pam, a 17-year-old student at the same high school. Apera has committed this offense. (An affirmative defense exists if the relationship began before employment and they are close in age).

33.021. Online Solicitation of a Minor

A person (17 or older), with intent to commit a listed sexual offense, communicates in a sexually explicit manner with a minor (under 17) online.

Punishment: 3rd Degree Felony (enhanced to 2nd Degree if minor is under 14).

Elements Checklist:

  • Is the actor `17 or older`?
  • Did they use the internet or electronic communication?
  • Did they have the `intent` to commit a sexual offense?
  • Did they communicate in a `sexually explicit` manner with a `minor` (under 17)?
Example: Forrest (18) chats online with Candy (12). The chats become sexual, and he intends to meet her for a sexual encounter. He has committed Online Solicitation of a Minor.

38.17. Failure to Stop or Report Aggravated Sexual Assault of a Child

A person observes the commission of an Aggravated Sexual Assault of a Child (under 14) and fails to assist the child or immediately report it to law enforcement, when they could do so without danger to themselves.

Punishment: Class A Misdemeanor

Elements Checklist:

  • Did the actor `observe` the commission (or attempt) of Continuous Sexual Abuse or Aggravated SA of a Child (victim < 14)?
  • Did they `fail to assist` the child or `immediately report` it to LE?
  • Could they have done so `without placing themselves in danger` of SBI or death?
Example: A person witnesses an adult sexually assaulting a young child in a public park. Instead of calling 911 or yelling for help from a safe distance, they walk away and do nothing.

Prostitution & Coercion

Prostitution & Solicitation

43.02. Prostitution

A person knowingly offers or agrees to receive a fee from another to engage in sexual conduct.

Punishment: Class B Misdemeanor (enhances with prior convictions).

Elements Checklist:

  • Did the person act `knowingly`?
  • Did they `offer or agree to receive a fee`?
  • Was the fee in exchange for engaging in `sexual conduct`?
Example: Guy advertises himself online and agrees to meet clients for sex in exchange for money. Guy is committing Prostitution.

43.021. Solicitation of Prostitution

A person knowingly offers or agrees to pay a fee to another person for the purpose of engaging in sexual conduct.

Punishment: State Jail Felony

Elements Checklist:

  • Did the person act `knowingly`?
  • Did they `offer or agree to pay a fee`?
  • Was the purpose to engage in `sexual conduct`?
Example: A client messages Guy online and agrees to pay him a fee for a "sex date." The client is committing Solicitation of Prostitution.
Promotion of Prostitution

43.03. Promotion of Prostitution

Acting other than as a prostitute, a person knowingly receives money from prostitution or solicits another to engage in prostitution.

Punishment: 3rd Degree Felony

Elements Checklist:

  • Was the actor `not` the prostitute receiving compensation for their own services?
  • Did they `knowingly` receive money from an agreement to participate in prostitution proceeds OR solicit someone for prostitution?
Example: Guy convinces his boyfriend, Ciro, to join him on a date. Guy collects the full payment and gives Ciro half. Guy is now Promoting Prostitution because he is receiving money from Ciro's prostitution.

43.04. Aggravated Promotion of Prostitution

A person knowingly owns, manages, or supervises a prostitution enterprise that uses two or more prostitutes.

Punishment: 1st Degree Felony

Elements Checklist:

  • Did the person `knowingly` own, invest in, finance, control, supervise, or manage a prostitution enterprise?
  • Did that enterprise use `two or more` prostitutes?
Example: Guy is now managing dates for Ciro and a new 17-year-old, Max. Because he is supervising a prostitution enterprise with two or more prostitutes, he is committing Aggravated Promotion.

43.031. Online Promotion of Prostitution

A person owns or manages an interactive computer service (website, app) with the intent to promote the prostitution of another person.

Punishment: 3rd Degree Felony

Elements Checklist:

  • Did the person own, manage, or operate a website/app?
  • Did they do so with `intent to promote` another person's prostitution?
Example: Abel sets up a website specifically for Guy and his associates to advertise and schedule their dates in exchange for 20% of the income. Abel is committing Online Promotion of Prostitution.
Coercion

43.05. Compelling Prostitution

A person knowingly causes another by force, threat, coercion, or fraud to commit prostitution; OR causes a child (under 18) by any means to commit prostitution.

Punishment: 1st Degree Felony

Elements Checklist:

  • Did the person `knowingly` cause another to commit prostitution?
  • Was it done by `force, threat, coercion, or fraud`? OR
  • Was the victim a `child under 18`? (If so, any means are sufficient).
Example: When Ciro tries to quit, Guy threatens to kick him out and cut off his drug supply, causing Ciro to continue committing prostitution. Guy is now Compelling Prostitution through coercion.

21.18. Sexual Coercion

A person intentionally threatens to commit a listed offense (e.g., Unlawful Disclosure of Intimate Material, Assault) to obtain a benefit in return (e.g., intimate visual material, a sexual act, or money).

Punishment: State Jail Felony

Elements Checklist:

  • Did the person `intentionally threaten` to commit a listed offense?
  • Was the purpose of the threat to `obtain a benefit` in return for not committing the offense?
  • Was the benefit `intimate material`, a `sexual act`, or `money`?
Example: A person tells their ex-partner, "If you don't send me naked pictures, I will post the old ones I still have." This is Sexual Coercion, because they are threatening Unlawful Disclosure to obtain new intimate material.

Visual & Recording Offenses

Recording & Disclosure

21.15. Invasive Visual Recording

Without consent and with intent to invade privacy, a person photographs or records a visual image of another's intimate area in a place with a reasonable expectation of privacy, or in any bathroom or changing room.

Punishment: State Jail Felony

Elements Checklist:

  • Was it `without consent` and with `intent to invade privacy`?
  • Did the person `photograph or record` a visual image?
  • Was it of an `intimate area` where the person had a reasonable expectation of privacy OR was it in a `bathroom or changing room`?
Example: Jack Ash drills a hole in the ceiling of his son's bedroom and uses a hidden camera to record his son and niece having sex. This is Invasive Visual Recording.

21.16. Unlawful Disclosure of Intimate Visual Material

Without consent and with intent to harm, a person discloses visual material of another's intimate parts or sexual conduct, knowing the material was created with an expectation of privacy.

Punishment: State Jail Felony

Elements Checklist:

  • Did the person `disclose` visual material?
  • Was it `without consent` and with `intent to harm`?
  • Did the material show intimate parts or sexual conduct?
  • Did the actor know the material was created under an expectation of privacy?
Example: After an ugly breakup, Art Teest emails naked pictures he took of his ex-girlfriend, Rosey Cheeks, to her coworkers, causing her great embarrassment.

21.19. Unlawful Electronic Transmission of Sexually Explicit Visual Material

A person knowingly transmits by electronic means sexually explicit visual material that was not requested by or consented to by the recipient.

Punishment: Class C Misdemeanor

Elements Checklist:

  • Did the person `knowingly transmit` visual material electronically?
  • Was the material `sexually explicit`?
  • Was it `unsolicited` (not requested or consented to by the recipient)?
Example: A person sends an unsolicited explicit photo to someone via a dating app or text message.
Child Pornography & Harmful Material

43.25. Sexual Performance by a Child

A person employs, authorizes, or induces a child (under 18) to engage in sexual conduct for a performance. Also applies to a parent who consents to it.

Punishment: 2nd Degree Felony (enhanced to 1st Degree if victim is under 14).

Elements Checklist:

  • Did the person employ, authorize, or induce a `child` (under 18) to engage in `sexual conduct`?
  • Was the conduct for a `performance` (play, photo, video, etc.)?
Example: Jack Ash knows his son and niece are having sex and allows it to happen in his home, partly because he is recording it. He has consented to and authorized their participation in a sexual performance.

43.26. Possession or Promotion of Child Pornography

A person knowingly possesses, or accesses with intent to view, visual material that depicts a child (under 18) engaging in sexual conduct.

Punishment: 3rd Degree Felony (and up, based on quantity and priors).

Elements Checklist:

  • Did the person `knowingly or intentionally` possess (or access with intent to view) visual material?
  • Did the material visually depict a `child` (under 18) engaging in `sexual conduct`?
  • Did the person `know` the material depicted a child in this way?
Example: On Jack Ash's computer, investigators find videos of his son and niece having sex, plus other videos of children in sexual acts that he received from others. He is in possession of child pornography.

43.24. Sale, Distribution, or Display of Harmful Material to Minor

Knowing material is "harmful" to minors, a person knowingly sells, distributes, or exhibits it to a minor (under 18).

Punishment: Class A Misdemeanor

Elements Checklist:

  • Did the person `know` the material was harmful for minors?
  • Did they `know` the recipient was a minor?
  • Did they `sell, distribute, or exhibit` the material to the minor?
Example: Forrest (18) shows adult pornography magazines and videos to his 13-year-old sister, Jenny.

Investigation & Reporting

Initial Response & Victim Rights

Best Practices for Initial Response

  • **Prioritize Safety & Medical:** Ensure the victim is safe and address any immediate medical needs.
  • **Offer a SAFE Exam:** Explain the benefits of a Sexual Assault Forensic Exam for health and evidence collection. It is free to the victim. They have up to 120 hours.
  • **Preserve Evidence:** Advise the victim not to eat, drink, shower, or change clothes if possible.
  • **Limited Initial Statement:** Conduct a brief interview in a safe place to get essential facts (crime elements, suspect ID, scene location). A full, detailed interview should happen later, preferably after at least two full sleep cycles.
  • **Provide Resources & Rights:** Give the victim your contact info, a case number, and the written notice of victim's rights required by CCP 56A.403.

Key Victim Rights

  • **Right to a Forensic Medical Exam:** Free of charge, within 120 hours, even without a police report.
  • **Right to an Advocate:** A sexual assault program advocate can be present during the exam and interviews.
  • **Right to a Pseudonym:** Under CCP Chapter 58, a victim can use a pseudonym in all public records.
  • **Right to Track Evidence:** Victims can anonymously track their evidence kit using the statewide "Track-Kit" system.
  • **Right to Protective Orders:** Victims may be eligible for an Emergency Protective Order (EPO) or a Sexual Assault Protective Order (SAPO).
Crime Scene & Report Writing

Crime Scene Checklist

  • Aid the victim and preserve evidence on their person first.
  • Secure and document the scene (photos/video).
  • Identify, collect, and preserve evidence (clothing, bedding, condoms, weapons, trace evidence).
  • Interview witnesses separately.
  • If a suspect is arrested, record spontaneous statements and keep them separate from the victim and scene. Preserve evidence on the suspect.

Key Principles for Reports

  • **Use Verbatim Quotes:** Use the victim's and suspect's exact words in quotation marks. Do not "clean up" or sanitize the language.
  • **Use Non-Consensual Language:** Describe the act with objective, forceful language (e.g., "he forced his penis into her vagina") rather than consensual terms ("they had sex").
  • **Document Lack of Consent:** "No" is communicated in many ways. Document actions like looking away, closing eyes, or physical resistance. If they couldn't resist, document what prevented them.
  • **Be Specific About Force:** Instead of "he threatened me," document the specific threats, tone of voice, and gestures.
  • **Do Not Fill Gaps:** If a victim's memory is fragmented, document it as such. Do not guess or fill in missing details.
