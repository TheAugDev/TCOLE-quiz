Robbery & Death Related Offenses

An Interactive Study Guide

Robbery Offenses (Chapter 29)

Characteristics of a Robber

  • Indicates a tendency to use violence on a stranger.
  • Bolder personality type; sacrifices secrecy for confrontation.
  • Can be classed by style: ambush (least planned) vs. planned operation.
  • May have a "signature" or M.O. (time of day, weapon used, phrases) that can link them to a series of crimes.
Robbery (PC 29.02)

A person commits Robbery if, in the course of committing theft and with intent to obtain or maintain control of the property, they:

2nd Degree Felony

  • Intentionally, knowingly, or recklessly cause bodily injury to another; OR
  • Intentionally or knowingly threaten or place another in fear of imminent bodily injury or death.

In the course of committing theft: means conduct that occurs in an attempt to commit, during the commission, or in immediate flight after the attempt or commission of theft.

Aggravated Robbery (PC 29.03)

A person commits Aggravated Robbery if they commit Robbery AND:

1st Degree Felony

  • Cause serious bodily injury to another; OR
  • Use or exhibit a deadly weapon; OR
  • Cause bodily injury to, or threaten/place in fear, a person who is 65 years or older or a disabled person.

Criminal Homicide (Chapter 19)

Types of Criminal Homicide

Criminal Homicide is intentionally, knowingly, recklessly, or with criminal negligence causing the death of an individual. All suspicious deaths should be treated as homicides until proven otherwise.

  • Anger Killing
  • Revenge or Jealousy
  • Triangle Killing
  • Killing for Profit
  • Random Killing
  • Drive-by Shootings
  • Murder-Suicide
  • Sex & Sadism
  • Felony Murder
Murder (PC 19.02)

1st Degree Felony

A person commits Murder if they:

  • Intentionally or knowingly cause the death of an individual; OR
  • Intend to cause SBI and commit an act clearly dangerous to human life that causes death; OR
  • Commit a felony (other than manslaughter) and in the course of it, commit an act clearly dangerous to human life that causes death (Felony Murder).

Sudden Passion: At the punishment phase, if the defendant proves by a preponderance of the evidence that they caused the death under the immediate influence of sudden passion arising from an adequate cause, the offense is reduced to a 2nd Degree Felony.

Capital Murder (PC 19.03)

Capital Felony

A person commits Capital Murder if they commit Murder (intentionally/knowingly causing death) AND certain aggravating circumstances exist, such as:

  • The victim is a known peace officer or firefighter.
  • The murder is committed during another felony (kidnapping, robbery, arson, etc.).
  • The murder is for hire (remuneration).
  • The murder occurs during an escape from a penal institution.
  • The victim is younger than 15 years of age.
  • The person murders more than one person.
Manslaughter & Criminally Negligent Homicide
Manslaughter (PC 19.04) - 2nd Degree Felony: Recklessly causing the death of an individual.
Criminally Negligent Homicide (PC 19.05) - State Jail Felony: Causing the death of an individual by criminal negligence.

Other Related Offenses

Offenses Involving Corpses
Abuse of Corpse (PC 42.08) - State Jail Felony: Without legal authority, knowingly disinterring, disturbing, damaging, concealing, or treating a human corpse in an offensive manner. Vandalizing the grave space is a Class A Misdemeanor.
Tampering With or Fabricating Physical Evidence (PC 37.09) - 2nd Degree Felony: If the item altered, destroyed, or concealed is a human corpse. The base offense for other evidence is a 3rd Degree Felony.
Other Death-Related Offenses
Tampering with Consumer Product (PC 22.09) - 2nd Degree Felony: Knowingly tampering with a consumer product to make it probable it will cause SBI. Enhanced to a 1st Degree Felony if SBI occurs.
Aiding Suicide (PC 22.08) - Class C Misdemeanor: With intent to promote or assist, aiding another to commit or attempt suicide. Enhanced to a State Jail Felony if the conduct causes suicide or an attempt resulting in SBI.
Failure to Report Felony (PC 38.171) - Class A Misdemeanor: Observing a felony where you believe SBI/death may have resulted and failing to immediately report it to law enforcement.
