Protective Orders & Related Laws

An Interactive Study Guide

Offenses Eligible for Protective Orders

Family Violence

Protective orders are available for victims of any offense involving family violence.

Stalking

Victims of Stalking (PC 42.072) are eligible to apply for a protective order.

Sexual Offenses & Trafficking

Includes victims of Sexual Assault, Aggravated Sexual Assault, Indecent Assault, and Human Trafficking.

Hate Crimes (Bias or Prejudice)

Victims of certain offenses (Assaults, Arson, Criminal Mischief, "Swatting") motivated by bias or prejudice based on race, religion, sexual preference, etc., are eligible.

Family Code Protective Orders (Chapters 81-88)

Filing & Temporary Ex Parte Orders
  • Who Can File (FC 82.002): An adult family/household member, a member of a dating relationship, or a prosecuting attorney/DFPS on behalf of a victim.
  • No Fee (FC 81.002): An applicant cannot be charged any fee by a clerk or law enforcement for filing, serving, or entering a protective order.
  • Temporary Ex Parte Order (FC 83.001): If a court finds a "clear and present danger of family violence," it can issue a temporary order without a hearing. This order is only enforceable once the respondent has been served and is typically valid for 20 days.
Duration & Out-of-State Orders

Duration of Final Order (FC 85.025):

  • A final protective order is effective for the period stated, not to exceed two years.
  • The duration can be longer than two years if the offender committed a felony FV offense, caused SBI, or was the subject of two or more previous orders.
  • If the offender is incarcerated when the order expires, the duration is extended until 1-2 years after their release date.

Out-of-State Orders (FC Chapter 88):

Law enforcement shall enforce a "foreign protective order" as if it were issued in Texas, provided it appears valid on its face (names parties, is in effect) and there is probable cause to believe it has been violated. A certified copy is not required for enforcement.

Code of Criminal Procedure Orders (Chapters 7B & 17)

Protective Orders for Non-FV Offenses (CCP Chapter 7B)

This chapter provides protective orders for victims of Stalking, Sexual Assault/Abuse, Indecent Assault, and Trafficking, regardless of their relationship to the offender.

  • Who Can File (CCP 7B.001): The victim, a parent/guardian of a minor victim, or a prosecuting attorney.
  • Duration (CCP 7B.007): Can be issued for the lives of the offender and victim or a shorter period. If no period is stated, it expires after 2 years.
  • Conditions (CCP 7B.005): The court can prohibit the offender from communicating, going near the victim's home/work/school, possessing a firearm, or tracking the victim.
Magistrate's Order for Emergency Protection (EPO) (CCP 17.292)

A magistrate may issue an EPO at a defendant's arraignment for offenses involving family violence, stalking, sexual assault, etc. The victim does not need to be present.

  • Mandatory Issuance: A magistrate SHALL issue an EPO if the family violence arrest involved SBI or the use/exhibition of a deadly weapon.
  • Duration: An EPO lasts for 31 to 61 days. If it was a mandatory issuance involving a deadly weapon, it lasts for 61 to 91 days.
  • Conditions: An EPO can include the same prohibitions as a final protective order, such as no-contact provisions and GPS monitoring.

Violations & Enforcement

Violation of Protective Order or Bond Condition (PC 25.07)

A person commits an offense if, in violation of a served order or condition of bond, they knowingly or intentionally commit certain acts.

Class A Misdemeanor

The base offense for violating a condition.

Example: A person subject to a protective order sends a harassing text message to the protected person.

3rd Degree Felony

The offense is enhanced if the person has two or more previous convictions for this offense, OR if they violate the order by committing Assault or Stalking.

Example: A person subject to a protective order goes to the victim's home and pushes them, causing pain (Assault BI). This is a 3rd Degree Felony, even for a first offense.

Important: A peace officer may not arrest the protected person for a violation of the order (e.g., for inviting the respondent over). Reconciliation does not invalidate the order.

Repeated Violation of Protective Order (PC 25.072)

3rd Degree Felony

A person commits this offense if, during a period of 12 months or less, they two or more times engage in conduct that violates a protective order under PC 25.07.

Example: In January, a person sends a harassing text (1st violation). In June, they drive by the victim's house (2nd violation). They can be charged with this 3rd Degree Felony.

Warrantless Arrest Authority & Detention

Warrantless Arrest (CCP 14.03):

  • An officer MAY arrest without a warrant for a PO violation not committed in their presence.
  • An officer SHALL arrest without a warrant for a PO violation committed in their presence.
  • An officer MAY arrest without a warrant for any offense involving family violence.

Detention after Arrest:

  • Detention after Posting Bond (CCP 17.291): The head of an agency may hold a family violence arrestee for up to 4 hours after bond is posted if there is probable cause to believe the violence will continue. A magistrate may extend this hold up to a total of 48 hours.
  • Notice of Release (CCP 17.29): Before releasing a FV or Stalking arrestee on bail, the law enforcement agency must make a reasonable attempt to give personal notice to the victim.
