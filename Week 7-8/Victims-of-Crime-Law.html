Victims of Crime: Law & Procedures

An Interactive Study Guide

Victims' Rights Framework

Texas Constitution - Article 1, Section 30

The Texas Constitution establishes the foundational rights for crime victims:

  • The right to be treated with fairness and respect for their dignity.
  • The right to be reasonably protected from the accused throughout the criminal justice process.
  • Upon request, the right to notification of court proceedings.
  • Upon request, the right to be present at all public court proceedings.
  • Upon request, the right to confer with a representative of the prosecutor's office.
  • The right to restitution.
  • The right to information about the conviction, sentence, imprisonment, and release of the accused.
General Rights of Crime Victims (CCP 56A.051)

This statute expands on the constitutional rights, providing a detailed list of entitlements:

  • Receive adequate protection from harm and threats.
  • Have their safety considered by the magistrate when setting bail.
  • Receive information about case status, court proceedings, and appeals.
  • Information about the Crime Victims' Compensation Fund.
  • Provide impact information for pre-sentence reports.
  • A separate or secure waiting area at all court proceedings.
  • Prompt return of property when no longer needed as evidence.
  • Notification to their employer if testimony requires absence from work.
  • Complete a Victim Impact Statement to be considered at sentencing and parole.
Privacy & Confidentiality

Right to Privacy (CCP 56A.101)

As reasonably practical, a victim's address and phone number may not be part of the public court file.

Confidentiality for Sex Offense Victims (CCP 58)

A victim of a sexual offense, trafficking, family violence, or stalking may choose a pseudonym to be used in all public files and records concerning the offense. The victim must complete and return a pseudonym form to the investigating law enforcement agency.

Key Definitions

Victim (CCP 56A.001)

A person who is the victim of sexual assault, kidnapping, aggravated robbery, trafficking, or injury to a child/elderly/disabled person; OR a person who has suffered personal injury or death as a result of the criminal conduct of another. Also includes guardians and close relatives of deceased victims.

Claimant (CCP 56B.003)

A person who is authorized to file a claim for compensation on behalf of a victim, a dependent of a deceased victim, or someone who voluntarily paid for a victim's medical or burial expenses.

Criminally Injurious Conduct (CCP 56B.003)

Conduct that occurs or is attempted, poses a substantial threat of personal injury or death, is punishable by fine/imprisonment/death, and does not arise from a normal motor vehicle accident (unless it was intentional or involved specific offenses like DWI or Failure to Stop and Render Aid).

Collateral Source (CCP 56B.003)

Any source of benefits or advantages that a claimant has received or is available to them for a pecuniary loss, such as restitution, insurance proceeds, or workers' compensation. This amount is typically deducted from a Crime Victims' Compensation award.

Law Enforcement Responsibilities

Victim Liaisons & Coordinators

Victim Assistance Coordinator (CCP 56A.201)

Designated by the District Attorney or county attorney. Their duty is to ensure that victims are afforded their rights under the law and to work closely with law enforcement and other justice system personnel.

Crime Victim Liaison (CCP 56A.203)

Each law enforcement agency shall designate one person to serve as the agency's Crime Victim Liaison. They consult with the Victim Assistance Coordinator to ensure victims receive their rights and information.

Required Written Notifications

Notification of Rights (CCP 56A.401)

At the first contact with a victim, a law enforcement agency shall provide the victim with a written notice containing:

  • Information about emergency and medical services.
  • The right to receive information about the Crime Victims' Compensation program.
  • The name, address, and phone number of the agency's Crime Victim Liaison.
  • The address and phone number of the prosecutor's Victim Assistance Coordinator.
  • A statement that they can call the agency for case status and information about their rights.
Other Duties
  • Notice of Release of FV Offenders (CCP 42.21): Before releasing a person convicted of a family violence offense, the holding entity must make a reasonable attempt to notify the victim of the imminent release.
  • Presence of Advocate (CCP 56A.351): Before a forensic medical exam of a sexual assault victim, medical personnel must offer the victim the opportunity to have a trained sexual assault program advocate present.
