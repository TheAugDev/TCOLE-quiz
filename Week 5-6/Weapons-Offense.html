Texas Weapons Offenses

An Interactive Study Guide (Chapter 46)

Key Definitions (PC 46.01)

Firearm
Device designed to expel a projectile by explosion/burning. Excludes antiques made before 1899.
Handgun
A firearm designed to be fired with one hand.
Location-Restricted Knife
A knife with a blade over 5.5 inches.
Short-Barrel Firearm
Rifle with barrel < 16", shotgun with barrel < 18", or any rifle/shotgun with overall length < 26".
Explosive Weapon
Any bomb, grenade, rocket, or mine designed to inflict SBI/death or substantial property damage.
Hoax Bomb
A device that reasonably appears to be an explosive or causes alarm to a public safety agency.

Unlawful Carrying & Possession

Unlawful Carrying Weapons (PC 46.02)

General Rule:

A person commits an offense (Class A Misdemeanor) if they intentionally, knowingly, or recklessly carry a handgun and are not on their own premises or in/en route to their vehicle/watercraft, especially if they are under 21 or have certain recent convictions.

The "Holster Rule"

In a motor vehicle or watercraft, a handgun must not be in plain view UNLESS the person is 21+ (or licensed) AND the handgun is in a holster.

Intoxication: It is an offense to carry a handgun while intoxicated unless on your own property or in your vehicle.

Unlawful Possession of Firearm (PC 46.04)

By a Felon (3rd Degree Felony):

  • A convicted felon cannot possess a firearm for 5 years after release from confinement or supervision.
  • After 5 years, they can only possess a firearm at the premises where they live.

By a Family Violence Offender (Class A Misdemeanor):

A person convicted of a Class A misdemeanor assault against a family member cannot possess a firearm for 5 years after release from confinement or supervision.

Places Weapons Prohibited (PC 46.03)

A person commits an offense (generally a 3rd Degree Felony) if they intentionally, knowingly, or recklessly possess a firearm, location-restricted knife, club, or prohibited weapon in certain places.

Prohibited Locations:

  • School or educational institution premises/vehicle
  • Polling place on election day
  • Government court or court offices
  • Racetrack
  • Secured area of an airport
  • Within 1,000 ft of an execution on day of execution
  • Business that derives 51%+ of income from alcohol (TABC 51% sign)
  • High school, college, or pro sporting event
  • Correctional facility
  • Hospital or nursing home
  • Amusement park
  • Open governmental meeting

Important Exceptions & Defenses:

  • Peace Officers: PC 46.02 & 46.03 generally do not apply to peace officers, whether on or off duty.
  • LTC at Airport: It is a defense for a License to Carry holder at an airport checkpoint if they immediately exit upon being notified they have a handgun. Police may not arrest unless they refuse to leave.
  • LTC at College: LTC holders can generally carry concealed on college campuses, but institutions can establish certain "gun-free" zones with proper 30.06 signage. Openly displaying a handgun on campus is an offense.
  • Vehicle Exception: The prohibition for being within 1,000 ft of an execution does not apply if the person is in their vehicle on a public road.

Prohibited Weapons & Other Offenses

Prohibited Weapons (PC 46.05)

It is an offense (generally a 3rd Degree Felony) to intentionally or knowingly possess, manufacture, transport, repair, or sell the following:

  • Explosive Weapon
  • Machine Gun
  • Short-Barrel Firearm
  • Armor-Piercing Ammunition
  • Chemical Dispensing Device
  • Zip Gun
  • Tire Deflation Device (State Jail Felony)
  • Improvised Explosive Device (IED)

Exception: Does not apply if the item (e.g., machine gun, short-barrel firearm) is registered on the National Firearms Registration and Transfer Record (NFA).

Other Key Offenses
Unlawful Transfer of Certain Weapons (PC 46.06): A Class A Misdemeanor to, for example, sell a firearm to an intoxicated person or a handgun to a child under 18 (this is a State Jail Felony).
Hoax Bombs (PC 46.08): Knowingly making, selling, or possessing a hoax bomb with intent to make another believe it's real is a Class A Misdemeanor.
Unlawful Possession of Body Armor by Felon (PC 46.041): A convicted felon possessing metal or body armor commits a 3rd Degree Felony.
Making a Firearm Accessible to a Child (PC 46.13): A Class C Misdemeanor if a child (<17) gains access to a readily dischargeable firearm due to criminal negligence. Becomes a Class A if the child discharges it and causes SBI/death.
