Communication Fundamentals

2 Mindset Modes for Policing

  • Verbal Persuasion: The primary mode, used for most interactions to gain voluntary compliance.
  • Recognize & React: A tactical mode entered when a threat is perceived (e.g., SAFER violation), requiring a shift from communication to force options.

Goal of Law Enforcement Communication

Generate Voluntary Compliance, Cooperation, or Collaboration

The 7-Step Professional Contact

This is the universal plan for all professional contacts.

  1. Introduction: Introduce yourself and your agency.
  2. Contact Reason: State the reason for the contact.
  3. Their Chance to Explain: Allow the person to justify, explain, or respond.
  4. ID Info: Request their identification information.
  5. Additional Info: Ask any follow-up questions needed.
  6. Evaluate & Make a Decision: Based on the information, decide on the course of action (warning, citation, arrest).
  7. Close the Contact: Professionally end the encounter.
Dr. Mehrabian's Communication Theory (7-38-55% Rule)

A message's impact is overwhelmingly based on HOW it is said, not just WHAT is said.

7%

Content (The Words)

38%

Voice / Paralinguistic (Tone, Pitch, Pace)

55%

Non-Verbal / Kinesics (Body Language)

Key Takeaway: Approximately 93% of a message is interpreted based on non-verbal cues and tone of voice. A negative tone or body language can completely negate well-chosen words.

Emotional Intelligence (EQ) in Policing

What is Emotional Intelligence (EQ)?

The ability to recognize, understand, and manage our own emotions, and to recognize, understand, and influence the emotions of others. High EQ is the foundation of effective de-escalation.

The 5 Universal Truths of Human Interaction

According to Dr. George Thompson (Verbal Judo), all people want to be:

  1. Treated with dignity and respect.
  2. Asked rather than told to do something.
  3. Told WHY they are being asked to do something.
  4. Given options rather than threats.
  5. Given a second chance.

Tactical Communication Models

P.A.C.E. - Elements to Control in Every Encounter
  • P Problem: Analyze and identify the actual problem. Be aware that it can change.
  • A Audience: Read everyone on scene. Identify allies and adversaries. Adapt tactics accordingly.
  • C Constraints: Recognize obstacles to communication, such as intoxication, mental crisis, anger, or scene distractions.
  • E Ethical Presence: An expression of self-control. Become who you need to be to handle the situation professionally.
Handling Verbal Abuse: Deflection & Redirection

When a person is verbally abusive or argumentative, use a two-part technique to regain control of the conversation without escalating.

1. Deflection (Use 2 Phrases)

Acknowledge their emotion to let them vent.

  • "I 'preciate that..."
  • "I understand that..."
  • "I hear what you're saying..."
  • "I'm sorry you feel that way..."

2. Redirection (Follow with "BUT...")

Bring the conversation back to the point.

  • "...BUT, for everyone's safety I need you to..."
  • "...BUT, I'm here because..."
  • "...BUT, I still need to see your ID."
Handling Voluntary Non-Compliance: The 5-Step Model

A tactical model for gaining compliance from a person who is refusing to obey lawful orders but is not yet a physical threat.

  1. Ask: (Ethical Appeal) Make a polite request. "Sir, would you please step out of the car for me?"
  2. Set Context / Explain Why: (Reasonable Appeal) Explain the reason for the request. "I need you to step out so I can be sure you don't have any weapons."
  3. Present Options: (Personal Appeal) Present their options, emphasizing the positive choice. "You can step out voluntarily, or I will have to remove you, and I don't want to do that."
  4. Confirm Refusal: (Practical Appeal) Confirm their decision. "So you're saying you will not step out of the car?"
  5. Act: (Determine Appropriate Action) Take the appropriate action based on their refusal.

Failure to Identify (PC 38.02)

When is it an Offense?

A person commits Failure to Identify if they...

  • Intentionally refuse to give their name, address, or DOB to an officer who has lawfully arrested them. (Class C Misdemeanor)
  • Intentionally give a false name, address, or DOB to an officer who has lawfully arrested OR lawfully detained them, or who believes they are a witness. (Class B Misdemeanor)

Fugitive Enhancement: If the person is a fugitive from justice at the time of the offense, the penalty is enhanced (Class C becomes B, Class B becomes A).

Winning the "Name Game": Practical Steps

When a person claims not to have an ID, an officer should take systematic steps to verify their identity.

  1. Confirm if they have a state ID/DL, just not with them. Confirm the state.
  2. Ask for other forms of ID: school/work ID, credit cards, insurance cards, bills, etc.
  3. Gather information for a computer check: Full name (confirm spelling), DOB, Race, Sex.
  4. Ask collateral questions at different times: Address, siblings' names, where they grew up.
  5. Run computer checks (DL/ID, warrants, CCH) and compare the physical description from the return to the person in front of you.
