Key Definitions (HSC 481.002)

Core Substance & Statutory Definitions
  • Drug:
    A substance (other than a device or its component/part) that is:
    • Recognized as a drug in the official United States Pharmacopoeia, Homeopathic Pharmacopoeia, or National Formulary
    • Intended for use in diagnosis, cure, mitigation, treatment, or prevention of disease
    • Intended to affect the structure or function of the body (not food)
    (HSC 481.002(16))
  • Controlled Substance:
    A substance, including a drug, adulterant, or dilutant, listed in Schedules I-V or Penalty Groups 1, 1-A, 1-B, 2, 2-A, 3, or 4. Includes the aggregate weight of any mixture.
    Does not include hemp or tetrahydrocannabinols in hemp.     (HSC 481.002(5))
  • Controlled Substance Analogue:
    A substance with a chemical structure or effect substantially similar to a controlled substance in Schedule I/II or Penalty Groups 1, 1-A, 1-B, 2, or 2-A. (HSC 481.002(6))
  • Counterfeit Substance:
    A controlled substance that, without authorization, bears or is in a container/label with an actual or simulated trademark, trade name, or identifying mark of a manufacturer/distributor/dispenser other than the actual manufacturer. (HSC 481.002(7))
  • Immediate Precursor:
    A substance designated by rule as a principal compound used or produced primarily for use in the manufacture of a controlled substance, or as an immediate chemical intermediary, or whose control is necessary to prevent/limit manufacture. (HSC 481.002(22))
  • Dangerous Drug:
    A device or drug unsafe for self-medication and not included in Schedules I-V or Penalty Groups 1-4. Typically, prescription drugs requiring a legend such as "Rx only." (HSC 483.001(2))
  • Marihuana:
    The plant Cannabis sativa L., its seeds, and every compound or preparation of the plant or its seeds.
    Does not include resin, mature stalks, or sterilized seeds.     (HSC 481.002(26))
  • Drug Paraphernalia:
    Equipment, product, or material used or intended for use in planting, manufacturing, packaging, concealing, or introducing a controlled substance into the human body. (HSC 481.002(17))
  • Controlled Premises:
    A place where original or other records required under this chapter are kept, or a place (factory, warehouse, etc.) where a person registered under this chapter may lawfully hold, manufacture, distribute, dispense, administer, possess, or otherwise dispose of a controlled substance. (HSC 481.002(4))

Action, Person, and Process Definitions
  • Possession: Actual care, custody, control, or management. (HSC 481.002(38))
  • Deliver: To transfer, actually or constructively, a controlled substance, counterfeit substance, or drug paraphernalia to another, regardless of agency relationship. Includes offering to sell. (HSC 481.002(8))
  • Delivery: The act of delivering. (HSC 481.002(9))
  • Distribute: To deliver a controlled substance other than by administering or dispensing. (HSC 481.002(14))
  • Distributor: A person who distributes. (HSC 481.002(15))
  • Manufacture: The production, preparation, propagation, compounding, conversion, or processing of a controlled substance (other than marihuana), by extraction, chemical synthesis, or both. Includes packaging/repackaging and labeling/relabeling. Does not include preparation/compounding by a practitioner as an incident to administering or dispensing in professional practice or research. (HSC 481.002(25))
  • Production: Includes the manufacturing, planting, cultivating, growing, or harvesting of a controlled substance. (HSC 481.002(43))
  • Dispense: The delivery of a controlled substance in the course of professional practice or research, by a practitioner or person acting under the lawful order of a practitioner, to an ultimate user or research subject. Includes prescribing, administering, packaging, labeling, or compounding necessary to prepare the substance for delivery. (HSC 481.002(12))
  • Dispenser: A practitioner, institutional practitioner, pharmacist, or pharmacy that dispenses a controlled substance. (HSC 481.002(13))
  • Administer: To directly apply a controlled substance by injection, inhalation, ingestion, or other means to the body of a patient or research subject by a practitioner (or agent in their presence), or by the patient/research subject at the direction and in the presence of a practitioner. (HSC 481.002(1))
  • Human Consumption: The injection, inhalation, ingestion, or application of a substance to or into a human body. (HSC 481.002(21))
  • Practitioner: A physician, dentist, veterinarian, podiatrist, scientific investigator, or other person licensed, registered, or otherwise permitted to distribute, dispense, analyze, conduct research with, or administer a controlled substance in the course of professional practice or research in this state; or a pharmacy, hospital, or other institution so licensed; or a person practicing in and licensed by another state as a physician, dentist, veterinarian, or podiatrist, with a current DEA registration; or an advanced practice registered nurse or physician assistant to whom a physician has delegated authority under the Occupations Code. (HSC 481.002(39))

Drug Categories & Symptoms

CNS Depressants

Function: Slows down the brain and body.

Examples: Alcohol, GHB, Rohypnol, Valium, Xanax.

Symptoms/OD Signs: Slurred speech, disorientation, drunken behavior without odor of alcohol. Overdose can cause shallow breathing, clammy skin, weak pulse, coma, and death.

Narcotic Analgesics

Function: Relieve pain, induce euphoria, and create mood changes.

Examples: Opium, Codeine, Heroin, Morphine, Methadone, Vicodin, OxyContin.

Symptoms/OD Signs: Droopy eyelids, depressed reflexes, dry mouth, "on the nod," constricted pupils. Overdose can cause slow and shallow breathing, clammy skin, convulsions, coma, and death.

CNS Stimulants

Function: Increases heart rate and blood pressure, increases alertness, causes excitement.

Examples: Adderall, Amphetamines, Cocaine, Methamphetamine.

Symptoms/OD Signs: Anxiety, euphoria, irritability, talkativeness, grinding teeth (bruxism), runny nose. Overdose can cause agitation, increased body temperature, hallucinations, and seizures.

Hallucinogens

Function: Distorts senses and perception.

Examples: LSD, Peyote, Psilocybin, MDMA (Ecstasy).

Symptoms/OD Signs: Hallucinations, dazed appearance, body tremors, paranoia, difficulty with speech. Overdose is unlikely but delusions can create dangerous or suicidal behavior.

Cannabis (Marihuana)

Function: Alters mind, increases heart rate, relaxes.

Examples: Marihuana, THC, some CBD oils, K2/Spice (synthetics are in PG 2-A).

Symptoms/OD Signs: Bloodshot eyes, body tremors, impaired time perception, increased appetite, burnt marihuana odor. No overdose, but can cause paranoia and psychosis.

Penalty Groups & Offenses

Penalty Group 1 / 1-A / 1-B
  • Penalty Group 1 (HSC 481.102):
    Opiates and opium derivatives, including:
    • Heroin
    • Cocaine
    • Methamphetamine
    • GHB
    • Ketamine
    • Oxycodone
  • Penalty Group 1-A (HSC 481.1021):
    Primarily LSD (Lysergic Acid Diethylamide) and its analogues.
    Punishments are based on number of abuse units.
  • Penalty Group 1-B (HSC 481.1022):
    Fentanyl, its derivatives, and analogues.

Offenses & Penalties (PG 1, 1-A, 1-B)

Manufacture or Delivery (HSC 481.112 - 481.114)

Conduct: Knowingly manufactures, delivers, or possesses with intent to deliver a controlled substance.

Example: Sells a baggie containing less than one gram of cocaine to an undercover officer. State Jail Felony under PG 1.

PG 1 (<1g): State Jail Felony
PG 1 (1g to <4g): 2nd Degree Felony
PG 1 (4g to <200g): 1st Degree Felony
View M/D Statutes
Possession (HSC 481.115 - 481.118)

Conduct: Knowingly or intentionally possesses a controlled substance without a valid prescription.

Example: Officer finds two grams of methamphetamine in a person's pocket. 3rd Degree Felony under PG 1.

PG 1 (<1g): State Jail Felony
PG 1 (1g to <4g): 3rd Degree Felony
PG 1 (4g to <200g): 2nd Degree Felony

Example: Sells a THC vape cartridge (PG 2) to a 16-year-old high school student.

Marihuana & Paraphernalia Offenses

Possession of Marihuana (HSC 481.121)

Conduct: A person knowingly or intentionally possesses a usable quantity of marihuana.

Example: An officer finds a plastic baggie containing three ounces of marihuana in a person's car during a lawful search.

2oz or less: Class B Misdemeanor
>2oz to 4oz: Class A Misdemeanor
>4oz to 5lbs: State Jail Felony
View HSC 481.121
Delivery of Marihuana (HSC 481.120)

Conduct: A person knowingly or intentionally delivers marihuana.

Example: A person gives a friend a quarter-ounce of marihuana with no money exchanged.

1/4oz or less (no remuneration): Class B Misdemeanor
1/4oz or less (with remuneration): Class A Misdemeanor
>1/4oz to 5lbs: State Jail Felony
View HSC 481.120
Possession or Delivery of Drug Paraphernalia (HSC 481.125)

Conduct: A person knowingly or intentionally uses, possesses with intent to use, or delivers drug paraphernalia.

Example: A person is found with a glass pipe that has burnt marihuana residue inside.

Possession: Class C Misdemeanor
Delivery: Class A Misdemeanor
Delivery by person 18+ to person <18: State Jail Felony
View HSC 481.125

Dangerous Drug Offenses (HSC Chapter 483)

Possession of Dangerous Drug (HSC 483.041)

Conduct: A person possesses a dangerous drug unless obtained from a pharmacist or practitioner via a valid prescription.

Example: A person is found with a bottle of Alprazolam (Xanax) that has someone else's name on the prescription label.

Class A Misdemeanor
View HSC 483.041
Delivery or Offer of Delivery of Dangerous Drug (HSC 483.042)

Conduct: A person delivers or offers to deliver a dangerous drug without proper authorization.

Example: A person sells their own prescription Xanax pills to a friend.

State Jail Felony
View HSC 483.042
Forging or Altering Prescription (HSC 483.045)

Conduct: A person forges a prescription, increases the quantity on a prescription, or obtains a dangerous drug using a forged, fictitious, or altered prescription.

Example: A person finds a blank prescription pad and writes a prescription for a dangerous drug, signing a doctor's name.

Class B Misdemeanor (Enhanceable to Class A with prior conviction)

Other Offenses & Procedures

The Overdose Defense

A defense to prosecution for possession offenses is available if the actor was the first person to request emergency medical assistance in response to a possible overdose of another person, remained on scene, and cooperated with medical/law enforcement personnel.

This defense is NOT available if:

  • At the time of the request, an officer was already in the process of arresting the actor or executing a search warrant.
  • The actor was committing another offense (other than simple possession).
  • The actor has a prior conviction under the Health & Safety Code.
Manufacture/Delivery of CS Causing Death or SBI (HSC 481.141)

If a person dies or suffers SBI as a result of using a controlled substance manufactured or delivered by the defendant, the punishment for the M/D offense is increased by one degree.

Example: A dealer sells fentanyl (PG 1-B, <1g, 3rd Degree Felony) to a person who then overdoses and dies. The dealer's charge can be enhanced to a 2nd Degree Felony.

View HSC 481.141
Uncorroborated Testimony

Dangerous Drugs (HSC 483.072): A conviction under Chapter 483 (Dangerous Drugs) may be obtained on the uncorroborated testimony of a party to the offense.

Controlled Substances (CCP 38.141): A defendant may not be convicted of a Chapter 481 (Controlled Substances) offense on the testimony of a person who is not a licensed peace officer acting covertly unless the testimony is corroborated by other evidence tending to connect the defendant with the offense.
