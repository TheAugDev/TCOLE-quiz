Sex Offenses

An Interactive Study Guide

Adult Victim Offenses

Sexual Assault (PC 22.011)

Conduct: An offense if a person intentionally or knowingly, without the other person's consent:

  • Causes the penetration of the anus or sexual organ of another person by any means;
  • Causes the penetration of the mouth of another person by the sexual organ of the actor; or
  • Causes the sexual organ of another person to contact or penetrate the mouth, anus, or sexual organ of another person, including the actor.

Definitions (PC 21.01):

  • Deviate Sexual Intercourse: Any contact between the genitals of one person and the mouth or anus of another; or penetration of the genitals or anus of another with an object.
  • Sexual Intercourse: Any penetration of the female sex organ by the male sex organ.

Enhancements & Defenses:

  • Enhancement (1st Degree Felony): If the victim was a person the actor was prohibited from marrying or engaging in sexual conduct with (PC 25.01/25.02).
  • Enhancement (State Jail Felony): If committed by a health care provider using improper reproductive material.
  • Defense (for child victim cases): The conduct consisted of medical care for the child.
  • Affirmative Defense (for child victim cases): The actor was the spouse of the child, or was not more than 3 years older than a child victim aged 14 or older.

2nd Degree Felony

This is the base offense level for Sexual Assault.

Aggravated Sexual Assault (PC 22.021)

Conduct: An offense if a person commits Sexual Assault AND one of the following aggravating factors is present:

  • Causes serious bodily injury or attempts to cause the death of the victim or another.
  • By acts or words, places the victim in fear of imminent death, SBI, or kidnapping.
  • Uses or exhibits a deadly weapon.
  • Administers a substance to impair the victim's ability to resist.
  • The victim is an elderly or disabled individual.
  • The victim is younger than 14 years of age.

Enhancements & Defenses:

  • "Super" Aggravated Enhancement: Minimum term of imprisonment is increased to 25 years if the victim is younger than 6, or younger than 14 when other aggravating factors are present.
  • Defense: The medical care defense from PC 22.011(d) applies.

1st Degree Felony

This is the offense level for Aggravated Sexual Assault.

View Penal Code 22.021
Indecent Assault (PC 22.012) Stackable

Conduct: An offense if a person, without the other person's consent and with the intent to arouse or gratify the sexual desire of any person:

  • Touches the anus, breast, or any part of the genitals of another person.
  • Touches another person with their own anus, breast, or genitals.
  • Exposes or attempts to expose another's genitals, pubic area, anus, buttocks, or female areola.
  • Causes another to contact the blood, seminal fluid, vaginal fluid, saliva, urine, or feces of any person.

Enhancements:

  • State Jail Felony: If the actor has a previous conviction for Indecent Assault, or if the actor is a health care provider and the act was committed during treatment.
  • 3rd Degree Felony: If the actor has a previous conviction for the SJF-level offense.

Class A Misdemeanor

This is the base offense level for Indecent Assault.

Child Victim Offenses

Indecency with a Child (PC 21.11)

Conduct: An offense if a person, with a child younger than 17:

  • Engages in sexual contact with the child or causes the child to engage in sexual contact.
  • With intent to arouse or gratify sexual desire, exposes their anus or genitals knowing the child is present, or causes the child to expose their own.

Definition of "Sexual Contact" for this offense:

  • Any touching (including through clothing) of the anus, breast, or genitals of a child with intent to arouse or gratify.
  • Any touching of the child's body with the anus, breast, or genitals of a person with intent to arouse or gratify.

Affirmative Defenses:

  • The actor was the spouse of the child at the time of the offense.
  • The actor was not more than 3 years older than the victim, did not use force, and was not a registered sex offender.

3rd Degree Felony

For exposing genitals with sexual intent.

2nd Degree Felony

For engaging in sexual contact with a child under 17.

View Penal Code 21.11
Continuous Sexual Abuse of a Young Child (PC 21.02)

Conduct: An offense if, during a period that is 30 or more days in duration, a person commits two or more acts of sexual abuse against a victim who is a child younger than 14 years of age.

Affirmative Defense:

  • The actor was not more than 5 years older than the victim, did not use force/threats, and was not a registered sex offender.

1st Degree Felony (25 to 99 years or Life)

This is the punishment for Continuous Sexual Abuse of a Young Child.

View Penal Code 21.02
Improper Relationship Between Educator and Student (PC 21.12) Stackable

Conduct: An offense if a public or private primary or secondary school employee engages in sexual contact, sexual intercourse, or deviate sexual intercourse with a person enrolled in the school where the employee works, or with a student they know is enrolled in any primary or secondary school.

Affirmative Defenses:

  • The actor was the spouse of the student at the time of the offense.
  • The relationship began before the actor's employment, and the actor is not more than 3 years older than the student.

2nd Degree Felony

This is the offense level for an Improper Relationship Between an Educator and Student.

View Penal Code 21.12
Online Solicitation of a Minor (PC 33.021) Stackable

Conduct: An offense if a person 17 or older, with intent to commit certain sex offenses (like SA, Indecency), intentionally communicates in a sexually explicit manner with a minor or distributes sexually explicit material to a minor online. Also an offense to solicit a minor to meet for sexual purposes.

Definitions:

  • Minor: A person younger than 17, or who the actor believes is younger than 17.
  • Sexually Explicit: Communication or material that relates to or describes sexual conduct as defined in PC 43.25.

Enhancements & Defenses:

  • Enhancement: Increased to the next higher category of offense if committed during school hours and the actor knew the minor was enrolled.
  • Defense: The actor was married to the minor, or was not more than 3 years older and the minor consented.

3rd Degree Felony

Base offense for communicating/distributing material.

2nd Degree Felony

For soliciting a minor to meet, or if the minor is under 14.

Pornography & Unlawful Recording

Invasive Visual Recording (PC 21.15)

Conduct: An offense if, without consent and with intent to invade privacy, a person:

  • Photographs or records a visual image of an intimate area of another person who has a reasonable expectation of privacy.
  • Photographs or records a visual image of another in a bathroom or changing room.
  • Promotes a recording known to have been made in violation of this statute.

Definitions:

  • Intimate Area: Naked or clothed genitals, pubic area, anus, buttocks, or female breast.
  • Changing Room: A room provided for changing clothes, like a dressing room or locker room.

State Jail Felony

This is the offense level for Invasive Visual Recording.

View Penal Code 21.15
Unlawful Disclosure/Promotion of Intimate Visual Material (PC 21.16)

Conduct: An offense if a person, without consent and with intent to harm, discloses visual material depicting another person with intimate parts exposed or engaged in sexual conduct, knowing the material was expected to remain private.

Affirmative Defenses:

  • Disclosure was in the course of lawful law enforcement, medical treatment, reporting crime, or legal proceedings.
  • The material depicted voluntary exposure in a public or commercial setting.

State Jail Felony

Sexual Performance by a Child (PC 43.25)

Conduct: An offense if a person, knowing the character and content, employs, authorizes, or induces a child younger than 18 to engage in a sexual performance. Also an offense for a parent/guardian to consent to such a performance.

Definitions:

  • Sexual Performance: Any performance (play, photo, dance, etc.) that includes sexual conduct by a child under 18.
  • Sexual Conduct: Includes sexual contact, actual/simulated intercourse, bestiality, masturbation, sadomasochistic abuse, or lewd exhibition of genitals.

Affirmative Defenses:

  • The actor was the spouse of the child.
  • The conduct was for a bona fide educational, medical, judicial, or law enforcement purpose.
  • The actor is not more than two years older than the child.

2nd Degree Felony

Base offense for employing/inducing a child.

3rd Degree Felony

Base offense for producing/directing/promoting the performance.

Possession or Promotion of Child Pornography (PC 43.26)

Conduct: An offense if a person knowingly or intentionally possesses, or accesses with intent to view, visual material that visually depicts a child younger than 18 engaging in sexual conduct, and the person knows the material depicts the child in such a manner.

Enhancements & Defenses:

  • Enhancements: Offense level increases based on the number of depictions possessed (100+ for F2, 500+ for F1) or for prior convictions. Further enhanced if the actor is a childcare employee or the victim is under 10.
  • Affirmative Defense: The conduct was for a bona fide educational, medical, psychological, judicial, law enforcement, or legislative purpose. Or, the actor is not more than two years older than the child.
  • Defense: For law enforcement or school administrators who possess the material in good faith as part of an investigation of a violation.

3rd Degree Felony

The base offense for possessing fewer than 100 depictions.

Prostitution & Related Offenses

Prostitution & Solicitation

Prostitution (PC 43.02): An offense if a person knowingly offers or agrees to receive a fee from another to engage in sexual conduct.

Solicitation of Prostitution (PC 43.021): An offense if a person knowingly offers or agrees to pay a fee to another person for the purpose of engaging in sexual conduct.

Enhancements & Defenses:

  • Prostitution Enhancements: Becomes a Class A Misdemeanor for 1-2 priors, and a State Jail Felony for 3+ priors.
  • Solicitation Enhancements: Becomes a 3rd Degree Felony for a prior, and a 2nd Degree Felony if the person solicited is under 18. Punishment is also increased if committed near a school.
  • Defense to Prostitution: The actor was a victim of human trafficking (20A.02) or compelling prostitution (43.05).

Class B Misdemeanor

Base offense for Prostitution.

State Jail Felony

Base offense for Solicitation of Prostitution.

Promotion & Compelling Prostitution

Promotion of Prostitution (PC 43.03): An offense if a person, acting other than as a prostitute, knowingly receives money from or solicits another for prostitution.

Aggravated Promotion of Prostitution (PC 43.04): An offense if a person knowingly owns, invests in, finances, controls, supervises, or manages a prostitution enterprise that uses two or more prostitutes.

Compelling Prostitution (PC 43.05): An offense if a person knowingly causes another by force, threat, or fraud to commit prostitution, or causes a child under 18 to commit prostitution.

Enhancements:

  • Promotion of Prostitution: Becomes a 2nd Degree Felony with a prior conviction, or a 1st Degree Felony if the person promoted is under 18.

3rd Degree Felony

Base offense for Promotion of Prostitution.

1st Degree Felony

Base offense for Aggravated Promotion & Compelling Prostitution.

Online Promotion of Prostitution

Online Promotion of Prostitution (PC 43.031): An offense if a person owns, manages, or operates an internet service with the intent to promote the prostitution of another.

Aggravated Online Promotion of Prostitution (PC 43.041): An offense if the conduct above involves five or more persons.

Enhancements:

  • Online Promotion: Becomes F2 with a prior or if it involves a person under 18.
  • Aggravated Online Promotion: Becomes F1 with a prior or if it involves two or more persons under 18.

3rd Degree Felony

Base offense for Online Promotion.

2nd Degree Felony

Base offense for Aggravated Online Promotion.

Other Offenses

Prohibited Sexual Conduct (PC 25.02)

Conduct: An offense if a person engages in sexual intercourse or deviate sexual intercourse with another person the actor knows to be a relative, including:

  • Ancestor or descendant (by blood or adoption)
  • Current or former stepchild or stepparent
  • Aunt, uncle, brother, sister, nephew, niece (whole or half blood, or by adoption)
  • First cousin

Enhancements:

  • 2nd Degree Felony: The offense is enhanced from a 3rd Degree Felony if committed with the actor's ancestor or descendant.

3rd Degree Felony

Base offense level.

Sexual Coercion (PC 21.18) Stackable

Conduct: An offense if a person intentionally threatens (including by coercion or extortion) to commit another specified offense (like Sexual Assault, Indecency, etc.) to obtain a benefit, such as intimate visual material or a sexual act.

Enhancements:

  • 3rd Degree Felony: If the actor has a previous conviction for Sexual Coercion.

State Jail Felony

Failure to Stop or Report Aggravated Sexual Assault of Child (PC 38.17)

Conduct: An offense if a person observes the commission of a Continuous Sexual Abuse or Aggravated Sexual Assault of a child under 14 and fails to assist or immediately report it to law enforcement, provided they could do so without placing themselves in danger.

Note:

  • This statute does not apply to a person who has a legal duty to act under PC 22.04(b), such as a parent or caregiver.
