Sex Offenses

An Interactive Study Guide

Key Definitions

General

  • Deviate sexual intercourse: Any contact between the genitals of one person and the mouth or anus of another, or penetration of the genitals or anus with an object.
  • Sexual contact: Any touching of the anus, breast, or any part of the genitals of another person with intent to arouse or gratify the sexual desire of anyone.
  • Sexual intercourse: Any penetration of the female sex organ by the male sex organ.
  • Intimate parts: The naked genitals, pubic area, anus, buttocks, or female nipple of a person.
  • Promote: To manufacture, issue, sell, give, provide, lend, mail, deliver, transfer, transmit, publish, distribute, circulate, disseminate, present, exhibit, or advertise, or to offer or agree to do any of the above.
  • Simulated: Explicit depiction of sexual conduct that creates the appearance of actual sexual conduct and during which a person engaging in the conduct exhibits any uncovered portion of the breasts, genitals, or buttocks.
  • Visual material: Any film, photograph, videotape, negative, slide, or any photographic reproduction, or any disk, diskette, or other physical or electronic medium that allows an image to be displayed or transmitted.

Obscenity & Harmful Material

  • Obscene: Material/performance that (A) appeals to the prurient interest in sex; (B) depicts or describes patently offensive sexual acts or lewd exhibition; and (C) lacks serious literary, artistic, political, or scientific value.
  • Material: Anything tangible that is capable of being used or adapted to arouse interest, through reading, observation, sound, or in any other manner (not including 3D obscene device).
  • Performance: A play, motion picture, dance, or other exhibition performed before an audience.
  • Patently offensive: So offensive on its face as to affront current community standards of decency.
  • Obscene device: A device including a dildo or artificial vagina, designed or marketed as useful primarily for the stimulation of human genital organs.
  • Harmful material: Material whose dominant theme appeals to the prurient interest of a minor, is patently offensive to prevailing standards, and is utterly without redeeming social value for minors.

Electronic Transmission & Child Pornography

  • Minor: A person younger than 18 years of age.
  • Produce (visual material): Any conduct that directly contributes to the creation or manufacture of the material.
  • Dating relationship: A relationship as defined by Family Code 71.0021(b).

Adult Victim Offenses

Sexual Assault (PC 22.011)

Conduct: An offense if a person intentionally or knowingly, without the other person's consent:

  • Causes the penetration of the anus or sexual organ of another person by any means;
  • Causes the penetration of the mouth of another person by the sexual organ of the actor;
  • Causes the sexual organ of another person to contact or penetrate the mouth, anus, or sexual organ of another person, including the actor;

Related Procedures & Victim Rights (CCP/Health Code):

  • Sexual Assault Exam Requirement (CCP 56A.352): A forensic medical exam must be offered and conducted if the assault occurred within the last 120 hours (5 days). View CCP 56A.352
  • Advocate Presence (CCP 56A.351): Medical personnel must offer the victim the opportunity to have a trained sexual assault program advocate present during the exam. View CCP 56A.351
  • Exam Without Law Enforcement Report (CCP 56A.353): The exam can be performed even if the victim has not reported the assault to law enforcement. View CCP 56A.353
  • Confidentiality (CCP 58): Victim information is confidential and protected from public disclosure. View CCP 58
  • HIV/STD Testing (Health & Safety Code 81.050): A court may order the defendant to undergo testing for communicable diseases if bodily fluids were exchanged. View HSC 81.050

Definitions:

  • Deviate sexual intercourse: Any contact between any part of the genitals of one person and the mouth or anus of another person; or penetration of the genitals or anus of another person with an object.
  • Sexual contact: Any touching of the anus, breast, or any part of the genitals of another person with intent to arouse or gratify the sexual desire of anyone.
  • Sexual intercourse: Any penetration of the female sex organ by the male sex organ.

Example: A person uses physical force to compel an acquaintance to engage in intercourse.

Without Consent: Conduct is without consent if the actor:

  • Compels the other person by physical force, violence, or coercion.
  • Compels by threat of force or violence, and the other person believes the actor has the present ability to execute the threat.
  • Knows the other person is unconscious or physically unable to resist.
  • Knows that due to mental disease or defect, the other person is incapable of appraising the nature of or resisting the act.
  • Knows the other person is unaware the sexual assault is occurring.
  • Intentionally impairs the other person's power to appraise or control their conduct by administering any substance without the person's knowledge.
  • Is a public servant who coerces the other person to submit or participate.
  • Is a mental health or health care services provider who exploits a current or former patient due to emotional dependency.
  • Is a clergyman who exploits a victim's emotional dependency on the clergyman's professional character as a spiritual adviser.
  • Is an employee of a facility where the victim resides (unless married).
  • Is a health care provider who, during assisted reproduction, uses donor material without express consent.
  • Is a coach, tutor, or caregiver who exploits the other person's dependency.

Enhancements & Defenses:

  • 1st Degree Felony: If the victim was a person the actor was prohibited from marrying or engaging in sexual conduct with (PC 25.01/25.02).
  • State Jail Felony: If committed by a health care provider using improper reproductive material without express consent.
  • 2nd Degree Felony: Base offense level for Sexual Assault.
  • Defense (for child victim cases): The conduct consisted of medical care for the child and did not include prohibited contact.
  • Affirmative Defense (for child victim cases): The actor was the spouse of the child, or was not more than 3 years older than a child victim aged 14 or older, and not otherwise prohibited.

2nd Degree Felony

This is the base offense level for Sexual Assault.

Aggravated Sexual Assault (PC 22.021)

Conduct: An offense if a person commits Sexual Assault AND one or more of the following aggravating factors is present:

  • Causes serious bodily injury or attempts to cause the death of the victim or another person in the same criminal episode.
  • By acts or words, places the victim in fear that any person will become the victim of trafficking, death, serious bodily injury, or kidnapping.
  • By acts or words occurring in the presence of the victim, threatens to cause any person to become the victim of trafficking, death, serious bodily injury, or kidnapping.
  • Uses or exhibits a deadly weapon in the course of the same criminal episode.
  • Acts in concert with another toward the same victim and same criminal episode.
  • With intent to facilitate the sexual assault, administers or provides a victim any substance capable of impairing their ability to appraise or resist the actâ€™s nature.
  • The victim is younger than 14 years of age.
  • The victim is an elderly or disabled individual.

Definitions:

  • Child: A person younger than 17 years of age.
  • Elderly individual: As defined by PC 22.04(c).
  • Disabled individual: A person older than 13 who is substantially unable to protect themselves or provide for their own care due to age, disease, defect, or injury.

Example: During a sexual assault, the actor threatens the victim with a gun, placing her in fear of imminent serious bodily injury or death.

Enhancements & Defenses:

  • "Super" Aggravated Enhancement: Minimum term of imprisonment is increased to 25 years if the victim is younger than 6, or younger than 14 when other aggravating factors (like SBI, threat, weapon) are present.
  • Defense: The medical care defense from PC 22.011(d) applies.

1st Degree Felony

This is the offense level for Aggravated Sexual Assault.

Indecent Assault (PC 22.012) Stackable

Conduct: An offense if a person, without the other person's consent and with the intent to arouse or gratify the sexual desire of any person:

  • Touches the anus, breast, or any part of the genitals of another person.
  • Touches another person with the anus, breast, or any part of the genitals of any person.
  • Exposes or attempts to expose another person's genitals, pubic area, anus, buttocks, or female areola.
  • Causes another person to contact the blood, seminal fluid, vaginal fluid, saliva, urine, or feces of any person.

Enhancements:

  • State Jail Felony: If the defendant has a prior conviction for Indecent Assault, or is a health care/mental health provider and the act was committed during treatment and was outside generally accepted practices.
  • 3rd Degree Felony: If the defendant has a prior conviction for the SJF-level offense above.

Note: The actor may be prosecuted under this section, another law, or both if the conduct also constitutes another offense.

Class A Misdemeanor

This is the base offense level for Indecent Assault.

Child Victim Offenses

Indecency with a Child (PC 21.11)

Conduct: An offense if a person, with a child younger than 17 years of age, whether the child is of the same or opposite sex and regardless of whether the person knows the age of the child at the time of the offense:

  • Engages in sexual contact with the child or causes the child to engage in sexual contact.
  • With intent to arouse or gratify sexual desire of any person, exposes their anus or any part of their genitals knowing the child is present, or causes the child to expose their own.

Related Procedures & Victim Rights (CCP/Other Statutes):

  • Protective Orders (CCP 7B): Victims of Indecency with a Child are eligible for protective orders regardless of relationship to the offender. View CCP 7B
  • Confidentiality (CCP 58): Victim information is confidential and protected from public disclosure. View CCP 58
  • Sex Offender Registration (CCP 62): Conviction requires registration as a sex offender. View CCP 62

Definitions:

  • Sexual contact: Any touching, including through clothing, of the anus, breast, or any part of the genitals of a child, or any touching of any part of the body of a child with the anus, breast, or any part of the genitals of a person, with intent to arouse or gratify sexual desire.

Example: An adult touches the genitals of a 15-year-old over her clothing with the intent to arouse his sexual desire.

Affirmative Defenses:

  • The actor was the spouse of the child at the time of the offense.
  • The actor was not more than 3 years older than the victim, did not use duress, force, or threat, and was not required to register for life as a sex offender or have a reportable conviction/adjudication under this section.

3rd Degree Felony

For exposing genitals with sexual intent.

2nd Degree Felony

For engaging in sexual contact with a child under 17.

View Penal Code 21.11
Continuous Sexual Abuse of Young Child or Disabled Individual (PC 21.02)

Conduct: An offense if, during a period that is 30 or more days in duration, a person commits two or more "acts of sexual abuse" against a victim who is a child younger than 14 OR a disabled individual.

Acts of Sexual Abuse Include:

  • Aggravated Kidnapping (with sexual intent)
  • Indecency with a Child (by contact)
  • Sexual Assault / Aggravated Sexual Assault
  • Burglary (with intent to commit a sex offense)
  • Sexual Performance by a Child
  • Trafficking of Persons
  • Compelling Prostitution

Related Statutes:

  • Sex Offender Registration (CCP 62): Conviction requires lifetime registration as a sex offender. View CCP 62
  • Protective Orders (CCP 7B): Victims are eligible for protective orders. View CCP 7B

Example: Over a period of six months, a person commits three separate acts of sexual assault against his 12-year-old stepdaughter.

Affirmative Defense (for child victim):

  • The actor was not more than 5 years older than the victim, did not use force/threats, and was not a registered sex offender.

1st Degree Felony (25 to 99 years or Life)

This is the punishment for this offense.

Improper Relationship Between Educator and Student (PC 21.12) Stackable

Conduct: An offense if a public or private primary or secondary school employee engages in sexual contact, sexual intercourse, or deviate sexual intercourse with a person enrolled in the school where the employee works, or with a student they know is enrolled in any primary or secondary school.

Note: The name of the student victim may not be released to the public. The name of the accused employee may not be released until indictment, except for specific reporting purposes.

Example: A high school football coach engages in a consensual sexual relationship with a 17-year-old cheerleader at his school.

Affirmative Defenses:

  • The actor was the spouse of the student at the time of the offense.
  • The relationship began before the actor's employment, and the actor is not more than 3 years older than the student.

2nd Degree Felony

This is the offense level for an Improper Relationship Between an Educator and Student.

Online Solicitation of a Minor (PC 33.021) Stackable

Conduct: An offense if a person 17 or older, with intent to commit certain sex offenses (like SA, Indecency), intentionally communicates in a sexually explicit manner with a minor or distributes sexually explicit material to a minor online. Also an offense to solicit a minor to meet for sexual purposes.

Example: A 40-year-old man sends sexually explicit messages to someone he believes is a 15-year-old girl, with the ultimate intent of meeting her to commit sexual assault.

Enhancements & Defenses:

  • Enhancement: Increased to the next higher category of offense if committed during regular school hours and the actor knew the minor was enrolled.
  • Defense: The actor was married to the minor, or was not more than 3 years older and the minor consented.

3rd Degree Felony

Base offense for communicating/distributing material.

2nd Degree Felony

For soliciting a minor to meet, or if the minor is under 14.

Employment Harmful to Children (PC 43.251)

Conduct: An offense if a person employs, authorizes, or induces a child to work:

  • In a sexually oriented commercial activity; or
  • In any place of business permitting, requesting, or requiring a child to work nude or topless.

Definitions:

  • Child: A person younger than 21 years of age.
  • Nude: Entirely unclothed, or clothed in a manner that leaves uncovered or visible through less than fully opaque clothing any portion of the breasts below the top of the areola (if female), or any portion of the genitals or buttocks.
  • Sexually oriented commercial activity: A massage establishment, nude studio, modeling studio, love parlor, or similar commercial enterprise primarily offering services intended to provide sexual stimulation or gratification to the customer.
  • Topless: A female child clothed in a manner that leaves visible any portion of her breasts below the top of the areola.

Example: The owner of a strip club hires a 17-year-old to work as a "topless" dancer.

2nd Degree Felony

Base offense level (child under 21).

1st Degree Felony

If the child is younger than 14 at the time of the offense.

Pornography & Unlawful Recording

Invasive Visual Recording (PC 21.15)

Conduct: An offense if, without the other person's consent and with intent to invade the privacy of the other person, the person:

  • Photographs or by videotape or other electronic means records, broadcasts, or transmits a visual image of an intimate area of another person if the other person has a reasonable expectation that the intimate area is not subject to public view.
  • Photographs or by videotape or other electronic means records, broadcasts, or transmits a visual image of another in a bathroom or changing room.
  • Knowing the character and content of the photograph, recording, broadcast, or transmission, promotes a photograph, recording, broadcast, or transmission described above.

Definitions:

  • Female breast: Any portion of the female breast below the top of the areola.
  • Intimate area: Naked or clothed genitals, pubic area, anus, buttocks, or female breast.
  • Changing room: A room or portioned area provided for or primarily used for the changing of clothing and includes dressing rooms, locker rooms, and swimwear changing areas.
  • Promote: To manufacture, issue, sell, give, provide, lend, mail, deliver, transfer, transmit, publish, distribute, circulate, disseminate, present, exhibit, or advertise, or to offer or agree to do the same.

Example: A person uses a hidden camera in a department store dressing room to record people changing clothes.

Notes:

  • Posting a sign that recording is in progress is NOT sufficient to establish consent.
  • If conduct constitutes an offense under this section and another law, the actor may be prosecuted under either or both.

Unlawful Disclosure/Promotion of Intimate Visual Material (PC 21.16)

Conduct (Disclosure): An offense if a person, without the effective consent of the depicted person and with the intent to harm, discloses visual material depicting another person with intimate parts exposed or engaged in sexual conduct, knowing the material was expected to remain private, and the disclosure causes harm and reveals the identity of the depicted person.

Conduct (Threat): An offense if a person intentionally threatens to disclose such material to obtain a benefit (e.g., money, another image, sexual act).

Conduct (Promotion): An offense if, knowing the character and content of the visual material, the person promotes it for publication on an Internet website or other forum they own or operate.

Definitions:

  • Intimate parts: The naked genitals, pubic area, anus, buttocks, or female nipple of a person.
  • Promote: To procure, manufacture, issue, sell, give, provide, lend, mail, deliver, transfer, transmit, publish, distribute, circulate, disseminate, present, exhibit, or advertise, or to offer or agree to do any of the above.
  • Sexual conduct: Sexual contact, actual or simulated sexual intercourse, deviate sexual intercourse, sexual bestiality, masturbation, or sadomasochistic abuse.
  • Simulated: Explicit depiction of sexual conduct that creates the appearance of actual sexual conduct and during which a person engaging in the conduct exhibits any uncovered portion of the breasts, genitals, or buttocks.
  • Visual material: Any film, photograph, videotape, negative, slide, or any photographic reproduction, or any disk, diskette, or other physical or electronic medium that allows an image to be displayed or transmitted.

Example: After a breakup, a person posts nude photos of their ex-partner on social media with the intent to cause them embarrassment and emotional distress.

Notes & Defenses:

  • It is NOT a defense that the depicted person created or consented to the creation of the visual material, or voluntarily transmitted it to the actor.
  • Affirmative Defense: Disclosure or promotion was in the course of lawful law enforcement, medical treatment, reporting unlawful activity, or legal proceedings; or the material depicted voluntary exposure in a public/commercial setting; or the actor is an interactive computer service and the material was provided by another person.
  • If conduct constitutes an offense under this section and another law, the actor may be prosecuted under either or both.

State Jail Felony

View Penal Code 21.16
Unlawful Electronic Transmission of Sexually Explicit Visual Material (PC 21.19)

Conduct: An offense if a person knowingly transmits by electronic means visual material that:

  • Depicts any person engaging in sexual conduct or with the person's intimate parts exposed; or
  • Depicts covered genitals of a male person that are in a discernibly turgid state;
  • And is not sent at the request of or with the express consent of the recipient.

Definitions: "Intimate parts," "sexual conduct," and "visual material" have the meanings assigned by Section 21.16.

Example: A person sends an unsolicited nude photograph via text message to a stranger.

Notes: If conduct constitutes an offense under this section and another law, the actor may be prosecuted under either or both.

Sexual Performance by a Child (PC 43.25)

Conduct: An offense if a person, knowing the character and content, employs, authorizes, or induces a child younger than 18 to engage in a sexual performance. Also an offense for a parent/guardian to consent to such a performance, or to produce/direct/promote such a performance.

Example: A photographer pays a 16-year-old to pose for photographs that include lewd exhibition of the genitals.

Affirmative Defenses:

  • The actor was the spouse of the child.
  • The conduct was for a bona fide educational, medical, judicial, or law enforcement purpose.
  • The actor is not more than two years older than the child.

2nd Degree Felony

Base offense for employing/inducing a child (F1 if child < 14).

3rd Degree Felony

Base offense for producing/directing/promoting (F2 if child < 14).

View Penal Code 43.25
Possession or Promotion of Child Pornography (PC 43.26)

Conduct: An offense if a person knowingly possesses, or accesses with intent to view, visual material that depicts a child younger than 18 engaging in sexual conduct. Includes computer-generated/AI-modified images if based on a real child's image.

Related Statutes:

  • Sex Offender Registration (CCP 62): Conviction requires registration as a sex offender. View CCP 62
  • Duty to Report (PC 43.27): Businesses that process visual material must report suspected child pornography to law enforcement. View PC 43.27

Example: A person downloads and saves a video file to their computer that they know depicts a child under 18 engaged in sexual conduct.

Enhancements & Defenses:

  • Enhancements by Quantity:
    • Fewer than 100 depictions: 3rd Degree Felony
    • 100 to 499 depictions: 2nd Degree Felony
    • 500 or more depictions: 1st Degree Felony
  • Further enhanced (up to 25-Life) if the actor is a childcare employee or was receiving state funds for the care of a depicted child.
  • Enhanced if the victim is under 10 or for prior convictions.
  • Affirmative Defense: Bona fide educational, medical, judicial, or law enforcement purpose. Or, actor is not more than two years older than the child.
  • Defense: For law enforcement or school administrators who possess the material in good faith as part of an investigation.

Prostitution & Related Offenses

Prostitution & Solicitation

Prostitution (PC 43.02): An offense if a person knowingly offers or agrees to receive a fee from another to engage in sexual conduct.

Solicitation of Prostitution (PC 43.021): An offense if a person knowingly offers or agrees to pay a fee to another person for the purpose of engaging in sexual conduct.

Example (Prostitution): A person on a street corner agrees to engage in sexual conduct with an undercover officer in exchange for $100.

Example (Solicitation): A person offers an undercover officer $100 in exchange for sexual conduct.

Enhancements & Defenses:

  • Prostitution Enhancements: Becomes a Class A Misdemeanor for 1-2 priors, and a State Jail Felony for 3+ priors.
  • Solicitation Enhancements: Becomes a 3rd Degree Felony for a prior, and a 2nd Degree Felony if the person solicited is under 18. Punishment is also increased if committed within 1,000 feet of a school.
  • Defense to Prostitution: The actor was a victim of human trafficking (20A.02) or compelling prostitution (43.05).

Class B Misdemeanor

Base offense for Prostitution.

State Jail Felony

Base offense for Solicitation of Prostitution.

View Penal Code 43.02 & 43.021
Promotion & Compelling Prostitution

Promotion of Prostitution (PC 43.03): An offense if a person, acting other than as a prostitute, knowingly receives money from or solicits another for prostitution.

Aggravated Promotion of Prostitution (PC 43.04): An offense if a person knowingly owns, invests in, finances, controls, supervises, or manages a prostitution enterprise that uses two or more prostitutes.

Compelling Prostitution (PC 43.05): An offense if a person knowingly causes another by force, threat, or fraud to commit prostitution, or causes a child under 18 to commit prostitution.

Example (Aggravated Promotion): A person manages an escort service with five employees who engage in prostitution, and the person takes a cut of the proceeds.

Enhancements:

  • Promotion of Prostitution: Becomes a 2nd Degree Felony with a prior conviction, or a 1st Degree Felony if the person promoted is under 18.

3rd Degree Felony

Base offense for Promotion of Prostitution.

1st Degree Felony

Base offense for Aggravated Promotion & Compelling Prostitution.

Online Promotion of Prostitution

Online Promotion of Prostitution (PC 43.031): An offense if a person owns, manages, or operates an internet service with the intent to promote the prostitution of another.

Aggravated Online Promotion of Prostitution (PC 43.041): An offense if the conduct above involves five or more persons.

Example: A person creates and runs a website that advertises the services of prostitutes, including their photos, contact information, and prices.

Enhancements:

  • Online Promotion: Becomes F2 with a prior or if it involves a person under 18.
  • Aggravated Online Promotion: Becomes F1 with a prior or if it involves two or more persons under 18.

Other Offenses

Prohibited Sexual Conduct (PC 25.02)

Conduct: An offense if a person engages in sexual intercourse or deviate sexual intercourse with another person the actor knows to be, without regard to legitimacy:

  • The actor's ancestor or descendant by blood or adoption
  • The actor's current or former stepchild or stepparent
  • The actor's parent's brother or sister (whole or half blood)
  • The actor's brother or sister (whole or half blood or by adoption)
  • The children of the actor's brother or sister (whole or half blood or by adoption)
  • The son or daughter of the actor's aunt or uncle (whole or half blood or by adoption)
  • First cousin

Definitions:

  • Deviate sexual intercourse: Any contact between the genitals of one person and the mouth or anus of another, or penetration of the genitals or anus with an object.
  • Sexual intercourse: Any penetration of the female sex organ by the male sex organ.

Enhancements:

  • 2nd Degree Felony: If the offense is committed with the actor's ancestor or descendant.
  • 3rd Degree Felony: For all other relationships listed above.

3rd Degree Felony

Base offense level (2nd Degree if with ancestor/descendant).

Sexual Coercion (PC 21.18) Stackable

Conduct: An offense if a person intentionally threatens (including by coercion or extortion) to commit another specified offense (like Sexual Assault, Indecency, etc.) to obtain a benefit, such as intimate visual material or a sexual act.

Example: A person threatens to file a false police report against someone unless that person agrees to perform a sexual act.

Enhancements:

  • 3rd Degree Felony: If the actor has a previous conviction for Sexual Coercion.

State Jail Felony

Failure to Stop or Report Aggravated Sexual Assault of Child (PC 38.17)

Conduct: An offense if a person observes the commission of a Continuous Sexual Abuse or Aggravated Sexual Assault of a child under 14 and fails to assist or immediately report it to law enforcement, provided they could do so without placing themselves in danger.

Example: A person witnesses their neighbor committing an act of aggravated sexual assault against a child but does not call 911 because they "don't want to get involved."

Note:

  • This statute does not apply to a person who has a legal duty to act under PC 22.04(b), such as a parent or caregiver.

Failure to Comply with Registration Requirements (CCP 62.102)

Conduct: An offense if a person is required to register as a sex offender and fails to comply with any requirement of CCP Chapter 62, including:

  • Failure to register a new address within 7 days of arrival in a municipality or county, or by the first date local law enforcement allows registration.
  • Failure to complete annual or 90-day verification as required.

Related Statutes:

  • Registration Timelines (CCP 62.051): Must register no later than the 7th day after arrival in a new municipality or county. View CCP 62.051
  • Verification Frequency (CCP 62.058): Lifetime registrants must verify every 90 days; others annually. View CCP 62.058

Offense Levels:

  • State Jail Felony: Duty to register expires under Article 62.101(b) or (c).
  • 3rd Degree Felony: Duty to register for life and required to verify registration once each year.
  • 2nd Degree Felony: Duty to register for life and required to verify every 90 days.
  • Enhanced to the next higher category for a prior conviction under this statute or for using fraudulent identifying information.

Example: A person required to register as a sex offender moves to a new county but fails to register with the local law enforcement agency within seven days of arriving.
